This room introduces the importance of **cryptography** in both cybersecurity and CTFs, showing how it protects data and is commonly used in real-world systems and challenges.

It covers the **two main types of cryptography** (symmetric and asymmetric), explains how **RSA works** and where it’s used, and introduces **two key exchange methods** used to securely share keys over networks.

Finally, it discusses the **future of encryption**, especially how **quantum computing** could impact current cryptographic systems, and highlights the need for some hands-on tool usage and independent research.

**Ciphertext** is the encrypted form of data, while **plaintext** is the original data before encryption (it could be text, images, or any file). A **cipher** is the method or algorithm used to encrypt and decrypt data, modern ones are cryptographic, but older/simple ones like the Caesar cipher also exist.

**Encryption** is the process of converting plaintext into ciphertext using a cipher. **Encoding** is not encryption, it’s just a way of representing data (like base64) and is easily reversible. A **key** is required to correctly decrypt ciphertext, and a **passphrase** is separate from the key and is used to protect it (similar to a password).

There are two main types of encryption: **symmetric encryption** (same key for both encryption and decryption) and **asymmetric encryption** (different keys for each). Attacks on cryptography include **brute force** (trying every possible key or password) and **cryptanalysis** (finding weaknesses in the underlying mathematics).

**Note:** Cryptography is a large and complex topic, and this content is only a basic introduction, focusing mainly on theory.
