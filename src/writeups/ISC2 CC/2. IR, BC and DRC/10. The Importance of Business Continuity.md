**Importance of Business Continuity**  
The main purpose of business continuity is to keep business operations running while the organization recovers from a major disruption.

**Need During Disruption**  
When a serious event disturbs the environment, the organization must know how to continue essential business activities.

**Role of Communication**  
Communication is a key part of the plan and should include multiple contact methods and backup numbers.

**Phone Tree**  
Many organizations use a phone tree so if one person is unavailable, others know who to contact next.

**Procedures and Checklists**  
Organizations should follow clear procedures and checklists so every person knows their role and no critical step is missed.

**Plan Activation**  
The first step during a disruption is to contact the right individuals and begin activating the business continuity plan.

**Management Involvement**  
Management must be involved because priorities may change depending on the situation.

**Authorized Personnel**  
People with proper authority must be available to carry out necessary actions, such as shutting down critical areas if needed.

**Critical Contact Information**  
The plan should include important contact numbers for supply chain partners, law enforcement, and other outside services.

**Support for Critical Infrastructure**  
In major disruptions or cyberattacks, special communication methods may be needed so hospitals and other critical services can continue essential operations.

**Simple Meaning**  
Business continuity is important because it helps an organization stay operational, communicate effectively, and protect essential services during a major disruption.
