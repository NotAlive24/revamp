- **Calculated Financial Exposure:** Organizations weigh the "cost of entry" (like a $10,000 bid) against a massive potential payout. If the potential loss is small enough to be absorbed without threatening the company's survival, the risk is accepted as a standard cost of doing business.
- **Zero-Tolerance and Redundancy:** In high-stakes environments like healthcare, where the "cost" of failure is human life, risk tolerance is non-existent. This drives heavy investment in **fail-safes and infrastructure** such as backup generators and multiple utility providers to eliminate the possibility of a single point of failure.
- **Aspiration-Driven Risk:** For entrepreneurs, decision-making is fueled by the **perceived value of the reward** (autonomy and profit). They accept high personal risk because the psychological and financial benefits of success outweigh the security of a traditional job.
