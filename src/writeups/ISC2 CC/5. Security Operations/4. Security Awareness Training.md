**Security awareness training** _(teaching employees how to recognize and prevent security risks)_ helps ensure everyone understands their **responsibilities** _(tasks they are expected to perform)_ and **accountability** _(being responsible for their actions)_ when protecting an organization’s systems and data.

It also helps identify **carelessness** _(lack of attention to security practices)_ and **complacency** _(feeling security risks are not important)_ that could create security risks.

---

## Three Learning Activities
Organizations use three main ways to teach security concepts:

### Education
**Education** _(learning general concepts and understanding security principles)_ helps people understand security ideas and apply them in real situations.

### Training
**Training** _(learning specific skills or procedures)_ focuses on teaching people **how to perform security tasks**, such as:

- recognizing phishing emails
- using secure passwords
- following security procedures

### Awareness
**Awareness** _(making people aware of security risks and why they matter)_ focuses on gaining attention and encouraging people to think about security threats and safe behavior.

---
## Key Idea
Before teaching detailed rules or skills, organizations often need to **raise awareness first** _(make people understand that security issues exist)_ so that employees pay attention to security practices.

---

**Main Idea:**  
Security awareness training ensures employees understand **security risks, responsibilities, and correct behavior** to help protect the organization.
