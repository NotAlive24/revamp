Logical access controls are electronic methods used to limit access to systems, data, and sometimes even physical assets or areas. Unlike physical access controls, they are not tangible barriers.

They help control who can access an asset even if a person already has physical access to it.

Common types of logical access controls include **passwords**, **biometrics** used on devices like phones or laptops, and **badge or token readers** connected to a system.

**Main Idea**  
Logical access controls use electronic tools to restrict access to authorized users only.
