**Security Controls**  
A control is a safeguard or countermeasure used to protect data and systems. Its main purpose is to preserve **confidentiality, integrity, and availability (CIA)**.

**CIA Triad**  
Confidentiality means keeping information private, integrity means keeping information accurate and unchanged unless properly modified, and availability means ensuring information and systems are accessible when needed.

**Access Control**  
Access control limits which subjects (users or processes) can access which objects (data, files, or resources) based on defined rules.

**Firewall as a Control**  
A firewall is a security control that helps block unauthorized access from outside the system. It can also help prevent sensitive internal information from leaving the network and being accessed by unauthorized people.

**Simple Meaning**  
Security controls are protections that help keep data private, accurate, and available while restricting improper access.
