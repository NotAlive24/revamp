Risk reduction depends on how effective a control is.

A control must match the current situation and the type of asset being protected.

Controls should be able to adapt when conditions or risks change.

The strongest control is not always necessary. The right control should provide enough protection without being excessive.

The cost of the control should match the value of what is being protected.

**Main Idea**  
A good control is one that fits the risk, adapts to change, and protects assets at a reasonable cost.
